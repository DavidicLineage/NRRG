# Definitions

This document defines the core terms of the recursive-recombinant framework as precisely and minimally as possible.

## Recursion

**Recursion** is generativity through self-referential reapplication of structure, rule, or output across sequence.

A recursive process preserves continuity by returning to its own prior form, prior result, or prior operational pattern and using that as the basis for further development.

### Key features
- self-return
- sequential carry-forward
- iterative deepening
- inheritance of prior structure
- continuity through patterned re-expression

### Recursion is not
- mere repetition without transformation
- generic feedback in every sense
- any process involving multiple steps

A process counts as recursive in this framework when later states are substantially generated through structured return to prior states, outputs, or inherited forms.

## Recombinance

**Recombinance** is generativity through composition of independently specifiable sources, where novelty arises from their interaction rather than from self-referential reapplication.

A recombinant process preserves plurality at the point of input and generates new structure through composition among distinct elements.

### Key features
- multiple distinct sources
- compositional interaction
- hybridization
- cross-line or cross-domain novelty
- emergent structure from combination

### Recombinance is not
- mere aggregation
- any interaction whatsoever
- recursion with multiple parameters
- simple juxtaposition without generative effect

A process counts as recombinant in this framework when independently specifiable inputs interact in a way that produces new structure not reducible to self-return alone.

## Recombination

**Recombination** is a concrete event, episode, or outcome in which distinct sources are composed into a new arrangement.

If recombinance names the mechanism-class, recombination names an instance of that mechanism in action.

## Nested Generativity

**Nested generativity** is the higher-order condition in which recursively matured structures become substrates for recombinant interaction, and recombinant products become substrates for further recursive inheritance, refinement, or stabilization.

This is the core architecture proposed by the framework.

A nested recursive/recombinant system does not merely recurse and recombine in parallel. It cycles between them such that each mode acts on the products of the other.

## Inheritance

**Inheritance** is the carry-forward of structure, relation, constraint, function, or behavioral tendency across sequential instantiations.

Inheritance is broader than biological heredity. In this framework it includes any process by which later forms retain actionable continuity with earlier ones.

## Distinct Source

A **distinct source** is an input retaining independently specifiable structure prior to composition.

This is the main criterion separating recombinance from generic composition.

Two sources count as distinct when they can be individuated before interaction, even if they later become tightly integrated.

## Composition

**Composition** is the bringing together of elements into a larger structure.

Composition is broader than recombinance. Not all composition is recombinant.

Recombinance is a narrower class in which:
1. the inputs are independently specifiable, and
2. novelty arises from their interaction.

## Constraint

**Constraint** is any limiting condition governing viability, coherence, admissible transformation, propagation, or resource use.

Constraints do not merely suppress outcomes. They shape the space of what can persist, develop, or remain coherent.

Examples may include:
- physical limits
- energetic limits
- syntactic admissibility
- semantic coherence
- institutional rules
- logical consistency
- external safety boundaries

## Selection

**Selection** is the filtering process by which some structures persist, propagate, stabilize, or dominate while others decay, fail, or disappear.

Selection may be natural, cultural, cognitive, computational, or engineered.

Selection is not identical with recursion or recombinance. It acts on their outputs.

## Invariant

An **invariant** is a property, relation, function, or structural signature that remains sufficiently preserved across transformation to support continuity of identity or lineage.

An invariant need not be absolutely unchanged. It must remain stable enough for the system to count as preserving something recognizable across recursive or recombinant transformation.

## Lineage

A **lineage** is a temporally extended chain of inherited structure linked by recursive continuity.

A lineage may be biological, linguistic, cultural, conceptual, or artificial.

## Hybrid

A **hybrid** is a structure produced through recombinant interaction among distinct sources.

A hybrid may be stable or unstable, fertile or sterile, coherent or incoherent.

## Stabilization

**Stabilization** is the process by which a structure becomes sufficiently coherent, repeatable, or viable to support persistence, reuse, or inheritance.

Recursive processes often perform stabilization on recombinant outputs.

## Novelty

**Novelty** is the emergence of new structure, function, relation, or behavior relative to a prior state or source set.

This framework does not assume that all novelty is recombinant, only that recombinance is a major generator of novelty.

## Coherence

**Coherence** is the degree to which a structure holds together as an organized whole under relevant constraints.

A highly coherent structure may still be wrong, harmful, or fragile under different conditions. Coherence is not truth or goodness.

## Fertility

**Fertility** is the capacity of a structure to generate viable descendants, viable transformations, or further productive developmental pathways.

A recombinant novelty may be impressive yet infertile. Fertility matters because durable emergence depends on more than one-step innovation.

## Recursive Lock

**Recursive lock** is a failure mode in which recursive continuity becomes excessive, suppressing productive novelty and trapping the system in stale attractors.

## Recombinant Noise

**Recombinant noise** is a failure mode in which recombinant novelty outpaces stabilization, yielding incoherent, unstable, or nonviable outputs.

## Median Freeze

**Median freeze** is a failure mode in which modulation over-favors safe continuity, producing coherence without sufficient exploratory variation.

## Extreme Shatter

**Extreme shatter** is a failure mode in which novelty pressure exceeds integrative capacity, producing fragmentation beyond recoverable coherence.

## Attractor Tyranny

**Attractor tyranny** is a condition in which one successful lineage or pattern becomes so dominant that it suppresses productive alternatives and captures selection pressure.

## False Fertility

**False fertility** is the appearance of generative promise in a structure that cannot actually support viable recursive inheritance, recursive refinement, or durable descendants.

## Memetic Capture

**Memetic capture** is a condition in which recursive repetition and recombinant novelty lock a population into self-propagating but harmful cultural or linguistic structures.

## Runaway Adaptive Sludge

**Runaway adaptive sludge** is a catastrophic condition in which recursive persistence, recombinant novelty, and selective propagation couple without superior constraint sovereignty, producing self-expanding, misaligned, or destructive generativity.

Gray goo is one vivid special case, not the only one.

## Modulation

**Modulation** is the governing process by which a system regulates when to recurse, when to recombine, and under what thresholds or pressures.

A serious nested generative system likely requires modulation to avoid recursive lock, recombinant noise, or runaway coupling.

## Working Distinction

The minimal working distinction of this framework is:

- **Recursion** preserves and deepens structure through self-return.
- **Recombinance** generates novelty through composition of distinct sources.
- **Nested generativity** arises when the products of each become substrates for the other.

That is the conceptual spine from which the rest of the framework develops.
