# Open Questions

This document records the major unresolved questions, attack surfaces, and research directions for the recursive-recombinant framework.

Its purpose is not to weaken the theory, but to prevent premature closure.

A framework with cross-domain ambition becomes dangerous to itself if it stops identifying its own uncertainty. These questions therefore mark the current frontier between what is proposed, what is plausible, and what remains unearned.

## 1. Is Recombinance a Real Mechanism-Class?

The most basic open question is whether **recombinance** genuinely identifies a distinct mechanism-class, or whether it is merely a sharpened restatement of existing ideas such as composition, synthesis, blending, or integration.

### Pressure point
If recombinance adds no explanatory precision beyond generic composition, then the term may be unnecessary.

### What must be shown
To justify the term, the framework must show that recombinance:
- excludes recursion cleanly
- requires independently specifiable sources
- names a generative pathway not captured precisely enough by broader terms
- improves analysis across multiple domains

### Current status
Promising, but not settled.

## 2. What Counts as a Distinct Source?

Recombinance depends on the notion of **distinct source**, but distinctness can be slippery.

### Pressure point
What qualifies as distinct in:
- genetics?
- language?
- culture?
- cognition?
- artificial reasoning?

Are two schemas in one mind distinct sources?
Are two versions of the same institution distinct?
Are two modules in one model distinct?

### What must be shown
The theory needs a usable criterion for source-distinctness that is:
- rigorous enough to constrain use
- flexible enough to travel across domains

### Current candidate
A source is distinct if it retains independently specifiable structure prior to composition.

That may still need sharpening.

## 3. Are Recursion and Recombinance Truly Distinct, or Only Analytically Distinct?

A major pressure point is whether these are genuinely separable operator classes or merely different emphases within a broader generative field.

### Pressure point
Recursive systems often contain recombinant inputs.
Recombinant systems often rely on recursively stabilized components.

If the two modes always interpenetrate, are they still usefully distinct?

### What must be shown
The theory does not need absolute separation.
It needs:
- explanatory separation
- functional discrimination
- identifiable differences in what kind of novelty each primarily produces

### Current status
The distinction appears real, but likely not absolute.

## 4. Is Genetics the Canonical Example or the Source-Template?

The framework currently treats genetics as the clearest natural embodiment of nested recursive/recombinant structure.

The stronger conjecture is that genetics is not just an example, but the **biological source-template** later echoed in language, culture, cognition, and artificial reasoning.

### Pressure point
This is a much stronger claim than simple analogy.

### Competing hypotheses
- **H1:** genetics is the source-template, and later domains are transformed continuations of the same deep architecture
- **H2:** nested recursive/recombinant generativity is substrate-general, and genetics is one especially vivid realization among several

### What must be shown
To support H1, the framework would need a clear continuity argument showing how later domains inherit or transform biological generativity rather than merely resemble it.

### Current status
H2 is safer. H1 is bolder and remains unproven.

## 5. What Role Do Mutation, Stochasticity, and External Shock Play?

The framework emphasizes recursion and recombinance, but many systems are also shaped by:
- mutation
- randomness
- noise
- external disruption
- spontaneous symmetry breaking
- environmental shocks

### Pressure point
If the framework underplays these, it may overstate its own explanatory reach.

### What must be shown
The theory needs a clearer account of:
- what novelty recursion and recombinance explain
- what novelty arises from stochasticity or exogenous disruption
- how these interact

### Current status
Open. This is one of the biggest areas needing clarification.

## 6. How Should Selection Be Positioned?

Selection currently appears as a filtering process acting on recursive and recombinant outputs.

### Pressure point
Is selection merely downstream, or is it constitutive of nested generativity itself?

In evolutionary systems especially, recursion and recombinance without selection do not obviously yield durable adaptive structure.

### What must be shown
The theory needs a more exact positioning of selection:
- external filter?
- coequal operator?
- environmental boundary condition?
- emergent pressure within the generative loop?

### Current status
Partially defined, not fully integrated.

## 7. What Exactly Is an Invariant?

The concept of **invariant** is carrying a lot of weight.

### Pressure point
How much transformation can occur before an invariant no longer counts as preserved?

What is preserved in:
- genetics? function, structure, lineage?
- language? meaning, intelligibility, syntax?
- culture? identity, legitimacy, role?
- cognition? self-coherence, topology, predictive utility?

### What must be shown
The theory needs a more exact account of:
- weak vs strong invariants
- functional vs structural invariants
- when invariant failure becomes lineage break

### Current status
Load-bearing but underformalized.

## 8. Is Durable Novelty the Right Central Problem?

The framework is currently organized around the problem of **durable novelty**.

### Pressure point
Is that the right central lens, or should the theory instead foreground:
- evolvability?
- adaptive continuity?
- structured emergence?
- inheritable variation?
- open-ended generativity?

### What must be shown
The framework needs to determine whether “durable novelty” is:
- the best unifying phrase
- one outcome among several
- too narrow
- too broad

### Current status
Strong candidate, not yet final.

## 9. What Would a Formal Model Look Like?

Right now the framework is conceptually strong but mathematically light.

### Pressure point
Without formalization, it risks remaining powerful language rather than mature theory.

### Possible directions
- operator algebra
- graph transformations
- evolutionary simulation
- category-theoretic mapping
- symbolic process models
- agent-based modeling
- state-transition systems

### What must be shown
A useful formal model would need to:
- distinguish recursive from recombinant operations
- represent inheritance and invariants
- model stabilization and failure
- allow nested cycling
- produce testable dynamics

### Current status
Unbuilt.

## 10. Can the Framework Generate Testable Predictions?

A strong theory must do more than unify intuitions.

### Pressure point
Can this framework predict outcomes that are not obvious without it?

### Possible predictive directions
- systems dominated by recursion should show compression and lock-in
- systems dominated by recombinance should show bursts of novelty and instability
- systems with governed nesting should show higher evolvability
- catastrophic failures should correlate with weak constraint under coupled recursion and recombinance

### What must be shown
Predictions need to become:
- measurable
- domain-specific
- falsifiable or at least pressure-testable

### Current status
Early and suggestive.

## 11. How Far Does the Framework Really Travel Across Domains?

Cross-domain elegance is a strength, but also a risk.

### Pressure point
Does the framework clarify each domain, or merely redescribe them in one language?

A useful cross-domain theory must preserve difference as well as reveal similarity.

### What must be shown
For each domain, the framework should answer:
- what does it explain better than existing language?
- what does it fail to explain?
- what domain-specific detail resists translation?

### Current status
Partially promising, still vulnerable to overextension.

## 12. Where Does MEA Fit Exactly?

MEA appears highly compatible with this framework, especially as a modulating principle between stabilizing and exploratory dynamics.

### Pressure point
Is MEA:
- a governor between recursive and recombinant modes?
- a higher-order scheduling logic?
- a selector between median stabilization and extreme exploration?
- a separate framework that merely overlaps?

### What must be shown
The relationship between MEA and the recursive-recombinant framework needs explicit articulation.

### Current status
Strong intuitive fit, not yet formally integrated.

## 13. Is the Temporality/Spatiality Mapping Insight or Overreach?

This is currently the boldest and most vulnerable branch.

### Pressure point
The idea that systems engage time primarily through recursion and space primarily through recombinance is philosophically powerful, but may overreach.

### Key uncertainty
Is this:
- a deep heuristic truth about cognition?
- a process-metaphysical insight?
- an interaction model?
- an accidental metaphor mistaken for ontology?

### What must be shown
The framework must sharply distinguish:
- heuristic utility
- phenomenological description
- ontological claim
- physical theory

### Current status
Provocative, highly provisional.

## 14. Does the Framework Risk Premature Grand Unification?

This may be the most important epistemic danger.

### Pressure point
The theory feels broad enough to become intoxicating.

If it starts being applied everywhere without strong local discipline, it may become a flattening abstraction rather than a true legibility engine.

### What must be shown
The theory must continue to say:
- where it works
- where it does not
- what remains unexplained
- what counts against it

### Current status
Live risk.

## 15. What Are the Nearest Existing Theoretical Neighbors?

The framework likely overlaps with existing work in:
- genetics
- complexity science
- systems theory
- cybernetics
- cognitive science
- memetics
- linguistics
- philosophy of process
- evolutionary theory

### Pressure point
Without a map of neighboring ideas, the framework risks:
- accidental reinvention
- overstating novelty
- missing useful tools
- failing to differentiate itself

### What must be shown
The theory needs a literature-facing comparison eventually:
- what is already known
- what is merely renamed
- what is genuinely new in the present framing

### Current status
Not yet mapped systematically.

## 16. What Is the Minimum Viable Safety Architecture for Design Use?

The framework has obvious design power and equally obvious misuse potential.

### Pressure point
If nested recursive/recombinant structure can be operationalized, then questions of governance become unavoidable.

### What must be shown
Any practical application would need serious work on:
- constraint sovereignty
- external adjudication
- kill conditions
- invariant monitoring
- anti-runaway limits
- degraded reproduction under uncertainty

### Current status
Recognized as necessary, not yet developed.

## 17. What Would Count as Disconfirmation?

A theory becomes more serious when it can state how it might fail.

### Pressure point
What evidence or reasoning would actually count against this framework?

### Possible disconfirmers
- inability to distinguish recursion from recombinance in any disciplined way
- failure of the framework to clarify more than one domain
- inability to identify preserved invariants
- inability to produce even weak predictive leverage
- demonstration that existing terms already do all the same work without loss
- evidence that the cross-domain mappings are merely superficial analogies

### Current status
Needs to be stated more forcefully.

## 18. Research Agenda

The next major questions for development are:

1. Can recombinance be justified as a distinct operator class?
2. Can source-distinctness be made rigorous?
3. Can the genetics-to-language/culture/cognition bridge be articulated without hand-waving?
4. Can invariants be typed and formalized?
5. Can a toy formal model be built?
6. Can domain-specific predictions be derived?
7. Can MEA be integrated explicitly?
8. Can the temporality/spatiality branch be bounded more carefully?
9. Can the theory say what would falsify or limit it?
10. Can the safety architecture be made explicit before design use?

## Closing Formulation

A seed theory remains alive only as long as it can still ask real questions of itself.

These open questions are not peripheral.
They are the current test of whether the recursive-recombinant framework is:
- a useful distinction,
- a deep operator theory,
- a cross-domain legibility engine,
or
- an elegant overgeneralization.

The answer will depend on whether the framework survives contact with these questions while continuing to increase explanatory precision.
