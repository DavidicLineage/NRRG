# Domain Mappings

This document maps the recursive-recombinant framework across the major domains currently under consideration.

The purpose of this file is not to prove equivalence among domains, but to test whether the same generative architecture appears in transformed form across distinct substrates.

The working question is:

> Where do recursion, recombinance, inheritance, selection, constraint, and invariants appear in each domain?

If these mappings hold, the framework gains cross-domain legibility. If they fail, the framework may be narrower than currently suspected.

## 1. Genetics

Genetics is the clearest natural example of nested recursive/recombinant generativity.

### Recursive aspect
Genetic systems recursively preserve structure through inheritance across generations. Traits, regulatory patterns, developmental instructions, and lineage continuity are carried forward through repeated reproduction and expression.

This is recursive in the framework sense because later biological states arise through structured return to inherited prior forms.

### Recombinant aspect
Genetic systems also generate novelty through recombination among distinct sources. Sexual recombination, crossover, fusion, duplication, domain shuffling, reassortment, and certain forms of horizontal transfer all exemplify generative composition among independently specifiable inputs.

### Nested structure
A lineage recursively preserves itself through time. Distinct lineages recombinantly interact. The resulting hybrids or altered structures then become substrates for further recursive inheritance and expression.

This yields durable novelty rather than mere one-off variation.

### Inheritance
- genes
- regulatory motifs
- developmental tendencies
- structural patterns
- lineage identity markers

### Constraint
- developmental viability
- energetic cost
- ecological conditions
- reproductive compatibility
- cellular and organismal coherence

### Selection
- reproductive success
- survival
- developmental viability
- environmental fit

### Invariants
Possible invariants include:
- retained function
- viable organismal structure
- regulatory coherence
- metabolic compatibility
- lineage continuity under transformation

### Core formulation
Genetics appears to instantiate nested recursive/recombinant structure directly:
recursive inheritance + recombinant variation + selective filtering.

## 2. Language

Language appears to reproduce the same broad architecture in symbolic and communicative form.

### Recursive aspect
Language is recursive through self-embedding, iterative elaboration, grammatical re-entry, repeated semantic inheritance, and patterned carry-forward of prior forms across usage.

A linguistic structure often develops by returning to existing constructions, meanings, or rules and extending them.

### Recombinant aspect
Language is recombinant through metaphor, phrase fusion, semantic crossing, lexical borrowing, genre mixing, analogy, construction blending, and compositional meaning-making among distinct sources.

Novel expressions often arise not by repeating one line, but by crossing multiple inherited elements.

### Nested structure
An inherited construction or phrase recursively stabilizes through repeated use. Distinct constructions, metaphors, semantic frames, or linguistic lineages recombinantly interact. The resulting hybrid expression may then stabilize recursively into usage, idiom, syntax, or discourse tradition.

### Inheritance
- words
- grammatical constructions
- semantic frames
- idioms
- rhetorical forms
- discourse conventions

### Constraint
- syntactic admissibility
- semantic coherence
- pragmatic intelligibility
- social acceptability
- communicative usefulness

### Selection
- uptake by speakers
- ease of reuse
- expressive power
- memorability
- prestige
- functional fit in discourse

### Invariants
Possible invariants include:
- communicability
- recognizable semantic core
- syntactic legibility
- pragmatic function
- retained relational structure

### Core formulation
Language appears to generate durable novelty through recursive elaboration and recombinant semantic fusion.

## 3. Culture

Culture may be the broadest social-scale expression of the same architecture.

### Recursive aspect
Cultural systems recursively preserve themselves through ritual, memory, pedagogy, habit, institution, tradition, law, and intergenerational transmission.

A culture persists by patterned re-expression of prior forms.

### Recombinant aspect
Cultural systems generate novelty through borrowing, imitation, translation, synthesis, trade contact, conquest, migration, ideological fusion, artistic hybridization, and institutional recomposition.

Distinct cultural structures meet and produce new formations.

### Nested structure
A cultural lineage recursively stabilizes itself through repetition and transmission. It then encounters distinct lineages or external inputs. Selected elements are recombinantly integrated. The resulting synthesis is recursively transmitted as a new continuity.

This pattern appears in religions, artistic schools, legal systems, political institutions, cuisines, technologies, and scientific paradigms.

### Inheritance
- traditions
- institutions
- narratives
- rituals
- norms
- values
- social roles
- symbolic systems

### Constraint
- legitimacy
- material conditions
- institutional inertia
- social coherence
- enforcement structures
- ecological and economic limits

### Selection
- adoption
- prestige
- survival under competition
- institutional persistence
- memetic spread
- practical success

### Invariants
Possible invariants include:
- group identity
- institutional function
- legitimacy structures
- symbolic continuity
- core narrative frames

### Core formulation
Culture appears to persist through recursive transmission while evolving through recombinant synthesis.

## 4. Cognition

Cognition may instantiate the framework at the level of mental structure and adaptive thought.

### Recursive aspect
Cognition is recursive through memory reinforcement, self-modeling, habit loops, reflective thought, iterative inference, narrative continuity, recursive evaluation, and repeated reactivation of prior schemas.

A mind often develops by returning to prior structure and elaborating it.

### Recombinant aspect
Cognition is recombinant through analogy, conceptual blending, cross-domain transfer, insight, creative synthesis, problem-solving by schema interaction, and hybrid model formation.

New thought often comes from interaction among previously distinct conceptual structures.

### Nested structure
A recursively stabilized model, memory, habit, or schema becomes available for recombinant interaction with other structures. The resulting insight or hybrid then undergoes recursive consolidation into memory, practice, identity, or further reasoning.

This allows both continuity of self and emergence of novelty.

### Inheritance
- schemas
- habits
- models
- memories
- narratives
- inferential tendencies

### Constraint
- working memory limits
- attentional limits
- coherence requirements
- embodiment
- emotional regulation
- task demands
- predictive utility

### Selection
- relevance
- usefulness
- emotional salience
- repeat success
- coherence with prior models
- problem-solving efficacy

### Invariants
Possible invariants include:
- self-coherence
- retained conceptual topology
- task relevance
- predictive success
- narrative identity

### Core formulation
Cognition appears to develop through recursive self-structuring and recombinant conceptual interaction.

## 5. Artificial Reasoning

Artificial reasoning systems may eventually require explicit treatment of the same architecture if they are to achieve robust, open-ended generativity without collapse.

### Recursive aspect
Artificial systems recurse through iterative refinement, self-correction, trace carry-forward, search loops, rule reapplication, recursive evaluation, and repeated transformation of prior outputs.

This includes systems that refine candidate structures through multiple passes.

### Recombinant aspect
Artificial systems recombine through multi-source synthesis, retrieval blending, modular composition, schema interaction, ensemble reasoning, tool integration, and hybrid candidate generation from distinct representations.

### Nested structure
A system recursively develops intermediate structures, then recombinantly composes them with external sources, modules, memories, or retrieved fragments. The resulting structures are recursively validated, compressed, stabilized, or elaborated into further reasoning steps.

### Inheritance
- state traces
- intermediate representations
- learned priors
- modular outputs
- persistent memory structures
- policy tendencies

### Constraint
- task specifications
- logical consistency
- compute limits
- alignment requirements
- verification standards
- external control boundaries

### Selection
- correctness
- utility
- task success
- external scoring
- verification pass/fail
- retained efficiency

### Invariants
Possible invariants include:
- semantic traceability
- logical validity
- task alignment
- preserved goal structure
- stable representational relations

### Core formulation
Artificial reasoning may require explicit governance of recursive refinement and recombinant synthesis to remain both creative and controllable.

## 6. Temporality and Spatiality

This is currently the most speculative mapping.

It should not yet be treated as established science. It is presented here as a philosophical and heuristic conjecture.

### Working conjecture
Systems appear to engage temporal reality primarily through recursive continuity:
- sequence
- carry-forward
- inheritance
- developmental re-expression
- memory
- persistence across succession

Systems appear to engage spatial reality primarily through recombinant relation:
- adjacency
- arrangement
- composition
- collision
- co-presence
- interaction among distinct elements

### Proposed nested structure
Living or cognitive systems may function as a mechanical differential between these two modes.

On this view:
- time is principally engaged through recursive continuity
- space is principally engaged through recombinant relation
- life or mind continually reticulizes between them

This would not mean recursion exists only in time or recombinance only in space. Both likely operate in both domains. The claim is about primacy of heuristic engagement, not absolute exclusivity.

### Inheritance
- temporal trajectories
- memory traces
- causal carry-forward
- developmental sequence

### Constraint
- embodiment
- causality
- resource limits
- spatial arrangement
- temporal irreversibility at lived scale

### Selection
- navigational success
- adaptive fit
- predictive utility
- survival of usable models

### Invariants
Possible invariants include:
- continuity of agent identity
- stable world-model relations
- preserved actionable structure across temporal and spatial change

### Core formulation
Temporality/spatiality may be the broadest philosophical expression of the recursive-recombinant distinction, but this remains conjectural.

## 8. Provisional Conclusion

These mappings suggest that nested recursive/recombinant structure may not be confined to one substrate.

Genetics appears to be the clearest direct embodiment.
Language, culture, cognition, and artificial reasoning appear to echo the same architecture under transformation.
Temporality/spatiality may represent the broadest philosophical articulation of the pattern, but that claim remains the least secure.

The framework stands or falls partly on whether these mappings continue to clarify rather than flatten the differences among domains.
